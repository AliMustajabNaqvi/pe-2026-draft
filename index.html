One mandate, with a real team behind it.

The supplier SPOC directive set the right goal — a single, coordinated commercial front. This lays out how to deliver it at HNL's scale: as a department with defined roles, defined process, and clear lines to Sales and Projects — not a single overloaded desk.

The case

The goal is right. The mechanism needs a team.

The directive correctly named a real problem. The fix is not to route everything through one person — it is to build the function that makes the goal achievable.

Problem 01

Scattered supplier contact

Multiple teams approaching the same principals independently dilutes leverage and confuses partners. → The function consolidates commercial contact behind one accountable owner.

Problem 02

Everything on one desk

A single contact for eight categories across every business unit is a bottleneck — and a single point of blame. → A department distributes execution while keeping governance central.

Problem 03

Product and Sales conflated

Positioning and pricing were bundled with bid-closing and customer ownership. → They are different disciplines: Product governs cost-in and the solution; Sales owns the customer and the close.

The mandate

We own the pre-award engine: supplier → solution → costing → handoff.

Product & Engineering is an enabling function. It does not book revenue — it makes Sales win and Projects deliver. It is the central interface to principals and suppliers, the home of technical solution design, and the custodian of product, pricing, and documentation.

How work flows

Centralised authority, distributed execution.

The directive's goal — leverage and consistency — is achieved by owning the commercial relationship, not by being the only person who ever speaks to a supplier. Engagement runs in three tiers, and the work moves through a defined lifecycle into Projects.

Tier 01

Routine technical contact

Project and technical teams engage suppliers directly for datasheets and spec clarifications — logged in the system. High volume, no queue.

Tier 02

Quotations & pricing

RFQs, pricing and commercial terms originate from or are approved by the function. This is where leverage lives, so this is where control sits.

Tier 03

Negotiation & partnerships

Strategic pricing, exclusivity and principal partnerships are led by the Engineering Manager and escalated through the matrix.

  • Schedule, budget and site delivery
  • Installation and commissioning
  • Project management and customer delivery

The PO transfers delivery — not the supplier relationship. Product & Engineering stays the technical and commercial authority on the equipment; Projects runs the project.

The lines

What this function is — and isn't.

Three boundaries stated up front, so they don't quietly re-blur once the structure is approved.

Peer, not report

Alongside Sales — not under it

Product sets the cost basis and the margin floor; Sales sets the final price within those guardrails and owns the customer. Two disciplines, one clean handoff. The function reports to the COO.

Scope ends at award

No project management

The function does not run delivery, sites or commissioning. At PO, scope transfers cleanly to Projects. Pre-sales design and post-award execution are not the same job.

Out of scope

Stripped of the misfits

Regulatory licence renewals — PEC, AEDB, ADB — return to compliance. The function owns product and engineering, not paperwork that was never its own.

How it's funded

An enabling function, funded by the teams it enables.

Product & Engineering is a shared-services cost centre, not a revenue line. Its cost should not sit as standalone overhead on one P&L — it should be allocated across the business units that benefit from it, so the dependency is visible and the function isn't read as discretionary overhead.

The model

Equal split — fixed and simple

The department's cost is divided in equal, fixed shares across the benefiting units — Sales, Projects and the sector business units. The split is set once and reviewed annually, not recalculated month to month. It spreads the cost fairly enough, makes every team a stakeholder in the function, and adds zero ongoing analysis or administrative overhead.

Why not usage-based? Tracking and reconciling each unit's consumption every month adds recurring hassle that outweighs the marginal gain in fairness. A flat, fixed split keeps funding a non-event.
The build

Phase it. Prove it. Scale it.

The optimum is the target, not a day-one demand. Stand the core up first, staffed from existing PPS people, and add depth as volume justifies it. Each step below carries its action points — the specific tasks, who owns them, and what they produce.

▸ Do this next — on approval
  1. COO approves the structure and the equal-split funding model.
  2. Confirm which PPS members fill the core roles — Engineering Manager, Solutions Engineer, Commercial Coordinator.
  3. Finalise and HR-approve the nine job descriptions.
  4. Publish the revised operating note under the SPOC directive.
  5. Brief Sales, Projects and the business units on the tiered engagement model.
STEP 01

Stand up the core

Get the function operational with the minimum team that can run the SPOC mandate, staffed from existing PPS people — no new hiring needed to start.

  • Approve the structure and equal-split funding
    Owner: COO · Output: signed operating structure
  • Slot the core roles from the PPS team
    Owner: Engineering Manager + HR · Output: named core team
  • Finalise and approve the nine job descriptions
    Owner: Engineering Manager + HR · Output: signed JD pack
  • Publish the operating note and tiered engagement model
    Owner: Engineering Manager · Output: circular issued
  • Stand up the quotation and engagement log in the dashboard
    Owner: Systems & Data · Output: live system of record
  • Brief Sales, Projects and the business units
    Owner: Engineering Manager · Output: rollout communicated
STEP 02

Add product-line depth

Bring on the product specialists as supplier volume and bid load justify them, line by line — depth where the work actually is.

  • Define the volume and bid-load triggers for each specialist
    Owner: Engineering Manager · Output: agreed hiring triggers
  • Assign or recruit the Power Generation specialist first
    Owner: Engineering Manager + HR · Output: line fully owned
  • Then Critical & DC Power, then Renewables & Storage
    Owner: Engineering Manager + HR · Output: full product coverage
  • Onboard each specialist against the supplier portfolio
    Owner: Engineering Manager · Output: specialist productive
STEP 03

Full enablement

Mature the knowledge and systems layer so the function scales without depending on individuals.

  • Stand up the central library and certification tracker
    Owner: Documentation & Training · Output: managed repository
  • Schedule supplier training and certifications
    Owner: Documentation & Training · Output: certified team
  • Migrate the dashboard to the hosted platform
    Owner: Systems & Data · Output: shared system of record
  • Formalise the annual funding-split review
    Owner: Engineering Manager + Finance · Output: agreed allocation

Lean or expanded is the COO's call. This document defines the optimum so the trade-offs of compressing it are explicit, not accidental.
